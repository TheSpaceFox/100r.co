As of June 19th 2021, we are still in the process of building our composting toilet. The first step was to remove old head, piping and the {thruhulls}.

We painted the area under the old marine head, and cut out a piece of the floor to accomodate the liquids tank. The tank needs to be lower to accomodate the height of the urine diverter. If we did not make a hole, the toilet would be too high for us to sit on.

For the liquids, we calculated that for two people for a full week, we would need a 31 liters tank. Of course, some days we don't urinate as much as other days, but that tank would allow for that odd week where we produce excess. We visited Industrial Plastics and Paints in Victoria and found a 27 liter (6 CDN Gal) tank. It is smaller than we had planned, but because we overcalculated the first amount we think that this will work out fine. We had them put a fitting overtop so we can close the tank while underway.

To empty the tank, we'll connect a foot pump to it to transfer it into a smaller, carry container to dump in public toilet, or in the sea when far from shore. When underway, we might just get a long hose and pump it directly overboard from the forward hatch, we are sure yet. We could have kept a thru-hull to empty it out, but preferred to close it.

We bought a Forian porcelain urine diverter by Shit and Blossoms to keep the liquids and solids separate. We chose porcelain because of its durable and hygienic properties. Very few companies offer the option of a porcelain separator, it is easy to clean and will last for a long, long time.

For solids, we got a 25 L tank with a cover (to close it in rough waters). For two people, this tank (we think) will only have to be emptied once every two weeks. For now, we are using wood chips sourced from a landscaping company in Victoria. We keep it in a bag with a scoop.

We are currently building a cover for the tank out of plywood. The shape of the head is very strange, cutting the wood support will be very difficult. We kept the toilet seat from our old marine head and will re-use it for our composting head.
